Define a ruler with $m = 4$ marks (e.g. $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$) so that the distances between any two marks are different. 
That is, if you can measure an integer distance  with  this  ruler,  there  should be only one way of making this measurement with your ruler.
Your ruler should be able to measure all the integer distances up to length $L = 6$.%(adapted from~\cite{memarsadeghi2016nasa})

\frame{\frametitle{Knowledge engineering in FOL}
\begin{block}{Knowledge engineer} 
investigates a particular domain, learns what conceptsare important, creates a formal representation of the objects and relations inthe domain 
\end{block}

\begin{block}{Knowledge engineering steps}
\begin{enumerate}
 \item \mat{Identify the task}: list compentency questions that the KB will respond
\item \mat{Assemble the relevant knowledge}: knowledge acuisition 
\item \mat{Decide on a vocabulary of predicates, functions, and constants}: translate the important domain-level concepts into logic-level names (i.e. \alert{ontology})
\item \mat{Encode general knowledge about the domain} axioms for all vocabulary terms
\item \mat{Encode a description of the specific problem instance} - atomic sentences about instances of concepts in the ontology
\item \mat{Pose queries to the inference procedure and get answers}
\item \mat{Debug the knowledge base}
\end{enumerate}
\end{block}
